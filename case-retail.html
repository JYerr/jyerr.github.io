Case 2024 · Retail · Operations

Inventory Turnaround at Scale

ClientNorthfield Retail Group
Footprint220 stores, national
Duration16 weeks
RoleLead consultant
−18%Inventory carrying cost
−32%Stockout rate
+6.4ptGross margin, affected SKUs
Situation

Warehouses full, shelves empty

Northfield was carrying record inventory value while stores still ran out of top-selling items. The forecasting model treated every SKU the same, so fast-moving items were chronically under-ordered while slow movers piled up in regional warehouses.

Approach

Segment, then re-forecast

  • Split the SKU catalog into four velocity tiers using 18 months of sell-through data
  • Built a tier-specific reorder model with different safety-stock rules per tier
  • Piloted the new model in 24 stores for 6 weeks before national rollout
  • Trained regional ops leads to override the model during known demand spikes
Signal
Inventory carrying cost, indexed to Month 0 (mocked data)
Impact

The tiered model freed up warehouse capacity and cut stockouts on the top 200 SKUs by a third. Northfield's ops team now owns the model and re-tunes tier thresholds quarterly.

"We stopped treating a phone case and a winter coat like they follow the same demand curve. That was the whole unlock." — VP Supply Chain, Northfield Retail Group (mocked quote)
