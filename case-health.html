Case 2023 · Healthcare · Operations

Cutting Patient Wait Times 24%

ClientBridgeview Health Network
Footprint6-clinic outpatient system
Duration10 weeks
RoleLead consultant
−24%Average patient wait time
+15%Daily patient throughput
+9ptPatient satisfaction score
Situation

Every clinic booked the same way, every clinic backed up differently

Bridgeview's six clinics used one shared scheduling template despite very different patient mixes. Two clinics were chronically overbooked in the morning and idle by 3pm; the rest ran the opposite pattern. Wait times were the top complaint in patient surveys for three straight quarters.

Approach

Rebuild the schedule around each clinic's real demand curve

  • Mapped hourly arrival patterns per clinic over 12 months of visit logs
  • Redesigned appointment slot lengths by visit type instead of a flat 20-minute default
  • Added a float-nurse pool that shifts between clinics based on the morning's actual load
  • Ran a 4-week A/B pilot across two clinics before rolling out network-wide
Signal
Average patient wait time, minutes, by week (mocked data)
Impact

Wait times dropped from a 41-minute average to 31 minutes network-wide, and the float-pool model became a permanent staffing pattern. Two other clinic groups in the same health system have since adopted the approach.

"The float pool sounded expensive on paper. It paid for itself in reduced overtime within the first month." — Director of Clinical Operations, Bridgeview Health Network (mocked quote)
