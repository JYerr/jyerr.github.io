Market Entry Strategy for SMB Lending

ClientSolace Pay
StageSeries B
Duration8 weeks
RoleLead consultant
$420MServiceable addressable market sized
3Priority segments identified
11wkTime-to-launch plan
Situation

Strong product, no clear beachhead

Solace Pay had a working SMB lending product but was spreading go-to-market spend evenly across every small-business vertical, with no segment converting fast enough to justify the spend. Leadership needed a defensible answer to "who do we sell to first."

Approach

Size the market, then rank the segments that can actually be won

  • Built a bottom-up TAM/SAM model across 14 SMB verticals using public filings and industry data
  • Scored each segment on loan approval economics, sales-cycle length, and competitive density
  • Interviewed 22 SMB owners across the top 5 candidate segments to pressure-test demand
  • Delivered a phased entry plan starting with the two highest-scoring segments
Signal
Projected revenue by entry phase, $K/month (mocked data)
Impact

Solace Pay reallocated 60% of its go-to-market budget to the two priority segments and launched the second segment two weeks ahead of the original plan. The scoring model is now used quarterly to evaluate new verticals.

"We'd been having the same debate about which segment to chase for two quarters. This gave us a number to argue about instead of a gut feeling." — Head of Growth, Solace Pay (mocked quote)
