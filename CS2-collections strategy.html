Collections Strategy — Personal Loans & BNPL

Segmenting a fast-growing loan book by early-warning risk, so collections effort lands on the right accounts before they're even past due — not after.

Situation

The problem

A fast-growing personal loan and BNPL book meant the collections team couldn't treat every past-due account the same way — there wasn't enough capacity to chase everyone equally, and chasing everyone equally wastes effort on accounts that would have cured on their own. The brief was to get ahead of risk before the due date, not just react after an account bounced.

Approach

What I did

  • Built a decision-tree based segmentation model using repayment history, cross-sell exposure, and acquisition-risk signals to classify accounts into priority tiers ahead of the due date.
  • Split the strategy into a pre-due track (proactive outreach on the highest-risk segment before an account was ever late) and a post-due track (reactive handling once an account had bounced).
  • Iterated the model from an account-level view to a relationship-level view — collections works at the level of the whole customer relationship, so an account that looked healthy on its own could still sit inside a risky relationship.
  • Separately designed a scorecard-based prioritization model for another portfolio, as a lighter-weight, continuously-updatable alternative where a full decision tree wasn't the right tool for the data available.

EXHIBIT 2 · Two methodologies, matched to the portfolio
MethodBest fit forHow it prioritizes
Decision tree Fast-growing book with rich behavioral history Splits accounts into discrete risk tiers via rule-based branching — easy to explain and act on operationally.
Scorecard Portfolio needing a simpler, continuously-updatable ranking Blends multiple risk signals into a single score, giving a smooth rank instead of discrete buckets.

Model choice depended on how much behavioral history was available and how the collections team needed to act on the output — not a one-size-fits-all decision.

Outcome

Result

Focusing pre-due outreach on the highest-risk tier cut bounce rates by roughly 20% and lifted recovery by around 15%, without a proportionate increase in collections headcount. The complementary scorecard model brought a similar lift to a second portfolio using a lighter-weight approach better suited to its data.

Takeaways

What I'd flag for next time

The model type mattered as much as the variables inside it — a decision tree earns its keep when there's enough clean behavioral history to build stable splits; a scorecard is the better call when you need something simpler to maintain and update. Matching the method to the portfolio was as important as any individual feature.
